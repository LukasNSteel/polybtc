## 4. What needs to be done (prioritized)

### P0 — Establish that the taker edge is real (blocking for live / scale)

**P0.1 — Run the faithful real-data backtest.**
The paper sim is the only evidence so far, and it *assumes* the very things that determine taker P&L. `backtest_parquet.py` / `replay_binance.py` replay decisions against real top-of-book ticks and settled outcomes — this is the right instrument.
- **Blocker:** the scripts expect `btc_ticks.parquet` and `btc_markets.parquet`; `research/data/` currently holds `snapshots.parquet` and `taker_trades.parquet` (not those files). **Acquire/build the tick + markets datasets first**, or repoint the scripts at the existing parquet.
- **Acceptance:** taker-leg P&L over ≥4–8 weeks of real ticks is positive net of fees under the committed latency/slack settings, with a per-trade edge distribution consistent with paper.

**P0.2 — Measure real tick-to-trade latency and fill capture.**
`taker_latency_ms: 420` and `speed_bump_ms: 250` are the single biggest P&L levers and are currently guesses. The whole "richened/gone" reject mechanism — i.e., your ~50% paper fill rate — is downstream of them.
- Add a **shadow logger**: when live (or in a tiny live probe), record submit timestamp, post-submit book, and actual fill/no-fill, to measure real latency and real fill-capture vs displayed size.
- **Acceptance:** empirical latency and capture replace the assumed defaults; if real latency ≪ 420ms, the takers are far more viable than paper suggests; if ≫, reconsider takers entirely.

### P1 — Tune, harden, and decide go-live

**P1.1 — Calibrate the new knobs from data, not intuition.**
- `limit_slack_ticks`: backtest showed diminishing returns past +2–3 ticks (the tail is runaway moves with no edge). Sweep 1/2/3 on the real-data backtest and lock the value.
- `pause_cooldown_sec: 90`: confirm it doesn't thrash (pause→probe→pause) in choppy regimes; consider exponential backoff if it does.
- `capture` / `race_loss_prob` / `feed_lag_ms`: recalibrate from the P0.2 shadow logs so paper stops lying about live.

**P1.2 — The principled limit fix (supersedes fixed slack).**
Fixed tick slack is a good patch; the correct design re-validates against **current fair at fill time**, not a stale signal ask — fill iff `fair_now − ask − fee > min_edge`. This captures the 80%+ of rejects where our fair richened with the book, without arbitrarily chasing N ticks. Prototype on the real-data backtest and compare to fixed slack.

**P1.3 — Go-live decision + capital ramp.**
- Decide paper → live cutover criteria (e.g., N days of positive paper *and* a positive real-data backtest *and* measured latency within tolerance).
- The in-flight `$1,000` sizing work implies a ramp. **Do not raise caps until P0 passes.** When ramping, scale `max_take`/`max_position`/kill-switch proportionally and re-verify the kill switch on the larger base.

**P1.4 — Concentration/variance risk.**
Paper shows single-market losses up to ~$43 against a $250 bankroll (~17%) — larger than `max_take_usd: 25`, implying snipe+scalp stacking or multi-take per market. Confirm intended, and cap **per-market total taker cost** explicitly if not.

### P2 — Engineering hygiene & observability

- **Commit the in-flight research** (5 untracked files) — currently at risk of loss; they’re core to P0.
- **Test runner:** tests are standalone scripts. Add a one-command runner (pytest wrapper or a `make test`) so `test_taker_fixes`, `test_speed_bump_exec`, and the others run in CI/pre-commit.
- **Monitoring/alerting:** surface pause/half-open transitions, kill-switch proximity, and live fill rate on the dashboard; alert on tier-3 entry and on kill-switch approach.
- **Reconcile equity semantics:** confirm paper baseline vs `seed_from_wallet` live equity is unambiguous in logs (earlier the same terminal showed $492 then $259 across runs — make the baseline source explicit in the status line).
